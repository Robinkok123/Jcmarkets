Risk Management
This is the foundation. If you protect capital, you stay in the game long enough for the edge to work.
The 1 to 2% Rule

Never risk more than 1 to 2% of your account on a single trade. Example: a $5,000 account means max $50 to $100 risk per trade.

Stop Loss, Always

Define the invalidation level before you enter. No stop means you are guessing and hoping.

Position Sizing

Position size = account risk divided by stop distance. If you risk $100 and your stop is 2% away, your position size is $5,000.

Risk to Reward

Aim for setups where potential upside is at least 2x your risk. A 50% win rate can be profitable with 2 to 1 risk to reward.

Daily Loss Limit

Set a max daily drawdown, for example 2 to 3R. If you hit it, you stop trading for the day.

Diversification

Avoid being overexposed to one narrative. Correlated positions can behave like one big bet.
